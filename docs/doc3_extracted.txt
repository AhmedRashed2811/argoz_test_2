Marketing campaigns, campaign types, budget, approval, and ROI tracking
Prepared for internal use — describes the business rules governing marketing campaign creation, marketing type configuration, finance approval, budget aggregation, and campaign performance tracking.

1. Introduction
1.1 Purpose
This document defines the functional business rules for the Real Estate CRM Marketing Management module. The module enables marketing teams to create campaigns, associate campaigns with real estate targets such as projects or units, select one or more campaign types, record detailed execution information, calculate the full campaign budget, route the campaign through finance approval, and monitor leads and ROI results after execution.
1.2 Scope
This specification covers campaign master setup, campaign type selection, Events, TV Ads, Street Ads, Social Media Ads, Exhibitions, total budget calculation, additional costs, finance approval statuses, campaign table columns, campaign lifecycle statuses, and marketing performance tracking. The document focuses on the marketing module and its functional relationship with lead attribution, but it does not define accounting, payment processing, media buying integrations, or advanced analytics implementation details.

2. Campaign Master Setup
2.1 Campaign Creation Objective
Marketing users create campaigns to organize all marketing activity around a defined business target. A campaign may be created to market a specific project, a specific unit, an exhibition presence, an event, a launch phase, or any other marketing objective defined by the company.
2.2 Campaign Master Fields

The display name used in lists, reports, approval screens, and lead attribution.

Start Date

The planned campaign start date. Used to calculate the Coming / Active / Ended campaign status.
End Date
Required
The planned campaign end date. Must not be earlier than the start date.
Campaign Target
Required / configurable
The object being promoted, such as Project, Unit, Exhibition, Event, or Other company-defined target.
2.3 Campaign Type Selection
After entering the campaign master fields, the user selects one or more campaign types. The selected types determine which detailed forms appear inside the campaign. The same campaign can include multiple types at the same time.
Supported campaign types: Events, TV Ads, Street Ads, Social Media, and Exhibition.
At least one campaign type must be selected before the campaign can be completed.
Each selected type opens its own detailed section and can contain one or more records where applicable.
All type-level and sub-section budgets are included in the final Total Budget section.
Key Rule: The campaign type selection is multi-select. For example, one campaign can include an event, a social media ad linked to that event, street ads supporting the same launch, and a TV ad running during the same campaign period.
2.4 General Validation Rules
Rule
Description
Date validation
Campaign end date must be equal to or after the campaign start date. Type-level date ranges should be validated against the campaign period or flagged according to company policy.
Budget validation
Budget values must be numeric, non-negative, and stored in EGP.
Multi-record validation
Events, TV Ads, Street Ads, and Social Media Ads may have more than one record under the same campaign.
Asset validation
Images and videos should be stored as campaign assets and linked to the specific type, channel, platform, or event record they belong to.
Audit validation
Budget changes, approval decisions, and approval reasons should be retained for traceability.

3.3.2 Street Ad Location Rules
For each selected street ad type, the user enters the total number of street ads and the budget for that type.
The user can add more than one location for the same street ad type.
Each location can have its own budget to support location-level cost analysis.
Bus Shelter and Transit / Bus Wrap do not require the same exact location entry rule as fixed outdoor placements; they may use area, route, vendor allocation, or company-defined tracking.
Budget Rule: Street ad budget contributes to campaign total as: street ad main budget + selected type budgets + location budgets, according to the company’s selected budget calculation rule.

3.5.1 Event Attribution for Social Ads
A social media ad may be created specifically to promote an event. In that case, the ad should be linked to the event record so the system can count attendees and/or leads generated from that ad. This improves ROI analysis by connecting platform spend to actual event attendance and sales pipeline impact.
The linked event should be selected from events under the same campaign.
Attendance and lead attribution should be recorded through lead source, event registration, QR code, landing page, manual reception entry, or any company-approved tracking method.
ROI reports should compare social platform budget against generated attendees, leads, and target KPI achievement.

The Total Budget section includes a button called Other Costs. When clicked, the system displays an additional repeatable section where users can record extra campaign costs that are not part of a specific campaign type.
Other Cost Field
Requirement
Description

Global Budget Rule: The user can add more than one Other Cost line. Each line is included in the calculated campaign total and remains visible for finance review.
4.3 Recommended Budget Calculation Formula
Campaign Total Budget = Sum of all selected campaign type budgets + sum of all repeatable sub-section budgets + sum of all channel/platform/location budgets + sum of all Other Costs.
5. Finance Approval, Campaign Status, and Campaign List
5.1 Finance Approval Global Rule
Every campaign has an Approval column controlled by finance approval. Approval status is independent from the date-based campaign lifecycle status. Each approval option includes a reason or note to keep the decision auditable.
Approval Status
Meaning
Reason Rule
Pending
Default / in review
Campaign has been created but finance has not made a final decision yet. Reason/note can describe what is still under review.
Approved
Finance approved
Finance approved the campaign budget and planned spending. Reason/note stores approval comments or conditions if needed.
Semi Approved
Partially approved
Finance approved part of the campaign, or approved with limitations. Reason is mandatory to explain what is approved and what is not.
Not Approved
Rejected
Finance did not approve the campaign. Reason is mandatory to explain the rejection reason.
5.2 Campaign Lifecycle Status
Campaign status is calculated from the campaign start and end dates and displayed in the campaign table.
Status
Rule
Coming
Current date is before the campaign start date.
Active
Current date is on or after the start date and on or before the end date.
Ended
Current date is after the campaign end date.
Important Distinction: Approval status and campaign lifecycle status are separate. A campaign can be Coming and Pending, Active and Approved, Active and Semi Approved, or Ended and Not Approved depending on dates and finance decision.

6. Lead Attribution and ROI Tracking
6.1 Campaign-to-Lead Relationship
The Marketing module should connect to the Lead Management module so every lead generated from a campaign can be traced back to the campaign and, where possible, to the specific campaign type or child record that generated it. This allows the campaign list to show lead counts and enables campaign performance reporting.
A lead generated from a campaign should store the Campaign reference.
Where available, the lead should also store the Campaign Type reference, such as Event, TV Ad, Street Ad, Social Media Ad, or Exhibition.
For social ads linked to events, the lead or attendee record should store both the Social Ad and the Event references.
The lead count in the campaign table should be calculated from attributed leads based on the selected campaign and reporting rules.
6.2 ROI and KPI Reporting
Metric
Functional Meaning
Total Budget
Final planned budget including all types and other costs.
Leads
Number of leads attributed to the campaign.
Cost per Lead
Total budget divided by attributed leads, where lead count is greater than zero.
Target KPI
The target value set on social media ads or campaign reporting screens.
Attendees
For events, the number of attendees captured through registration, reception, QR, or manual tracking.
Cost per Attendee
Event-related or social-event budget divided by attributed attendees, where attendee count is greater than zero.
Platform Performance
Budget and KPI view per selected social platform.
Reporting Rule: ROI calculations should be transparent and based on stored campaign budgets and attributed leads/attendees. The system should avoid mixing unrelated campaign costs unless the report explicitly uses total campaign budget.
